About Gleam

A board for the feeling
you can't describe yet.

Gleam is a vision board tool built for people who think in images — not in to-do lists. It started as a personal project, and grew into something people actually kept coming back to.

Our mission

To give every fleeting mood, half-formed idea, and quiet aspiration a place to live — so you can finally see what you've been imagining.

The story

Gleam started with a simple frustration: Pinterest felt like a search engine, not a personal space. Every tool for making moodboards either forced you into a grid, required a design background, or filled up with other people's content.

The idea was straightforward — build something that worked like an actual wall. Where you could take your own photos, arrange them however felt right, and come back to it the next day without anything having changed.

"No templates. No grid. Just your photos, and a blank wall."

Gleam is built to stay out of the way. The tools are there when you need them. The board is there when you don't. And the whole thing lives in your browser — no account needed to start.

What we believe

Mood first, tools second.

The board should feel like yours before it feels like software. We build features that stay quiet until you actually need them.

Your photos, not ours.

Gleam is built around images you already have — not a library of stock photos. The best moodboard is made from things that already mean something to you.

Start before you're ready.

No account needed for your first board. No templates to fill in. If you have an idea and a few photos, that's enough to start.
